IGCSE Computer Science Practice Test 3

Security Mitigation and Threats

Maximum Marks: 40
Recommended Time: 45 Minutes

SECTION A: Network Resilience and Attack Mitigation

Scenario: GameStream Pro is a popular online platform that hosts massive multiplayer games. Their primary concern is keeping their servers online.

Question 1: Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)

a) ...requests flooded their main server, slowing down service...

i. Name this type of attack and explain how it prevents legitimate users from accessing the server. [2]

ii. Explain why this type of attack is described as 'Distributed'. [1]

b) The company uses a firewall...

i. Describe the main function of a firewall in relation to the traffic entering and leaving the server. [1]

ii. State one limitation of a firewall regarding employee actions that could still lead to a security breach. [1]

Question 2: Brute Force and Password Security

GameStream Pro requires complex passwords to access source code repositories.

a) Define what a brute force attack is and explain the mechanism it uses to crack a password. [2]

b) Explain how the use of a strong password mitigates the risk of a successful brute force attack. [1]

c) State three specific criteria that define a strong password. [3]

SECTION B: Malware and Social Engineering

Scenario: Global Bank Corp trains its employees extensively on recognising and avoiding sophisticated cyber threats.

Question 3: Malware Identification and Prevention

a) An employee notices an excessive number of unwanted advertising pop-ups...

i. Name the type of malware responsible for flooding the end-user with unwanted advertising. [1]

ii. State two issues that this type of malware can cause, even if it is not immediately harmful. [2]

b) Another form of malware, known as spyware, is used to gather sensitive information.

i. Explain how spyware obtains personal data from a user's computer. [1]

ii. Describe two specific protective features that anti-spyware software might offer (other than removal). [2]

Question 4: Phishing and Social Manipulation

a) An attacker sends an email to a bank manager claiming to be from the internal IT department...

i. Name the broad category of cyber threat that relies on the manipulation of people. [1]

ii. Name the specific human emotion this threat relies upon... [1]

b) State three specific clues (related to communication content or address) that indicate this email is a potential phishing scam. [3]

c) Explain the difference between phishing and spear phishing. [2]

Question 5: Access Levels and Authentication

c) The hospital is considering using voice recognition...

i. State one benefit of using voice recognition. [1]

ii. State one major drawback of using voice recognition that could affect its accuracy. [1]
